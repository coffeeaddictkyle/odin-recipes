Ichiran Japanese Tonkotsu Ramen

Ichiran ramen's distinct flavor profile stems from its signature tonkotsu broth, meticulously simmered for hours using pork bones and collagen. This broth forms the foundation of the ramen, creating a creamy and umami-rich base.

Ingredients

  • 1 package of Ichiran ramen noodles
  • 16 oz. of Tokotsu broth
  • 5 oz. of sliced Chashu Pork
  • 1 egg
  • 1 scallion
  • 4 oz. of bean sprouts
  • 1 stalk of bok choy
  • 17 oz. of water
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • a pinch of sesame seeds
  • a pinch of chili flakes

Steps

  1. Boil about 16 oz. of water
    1. Slice the bok choy in half and blanch in the boiling water for 30 seconds
    2. Using the same water blanch the bean sprouts for 30 seconds
    3. Using the same water again cook the noodles for 2 minutes
      • When done rinse the noodles with cold water
  2. Heat the broth until simmer
  3. Add salt and pepper to taste
  4. Soft boil the egg
  5. Cut the egg in half
  6. Slice some of the green ends of the scallion for garnish
  7. Add the noodles, pork, bok choy, bean sprouts, scallions, and egg to the broth
  8. Add a pinch of chili flakes and sesame seeds to taste
