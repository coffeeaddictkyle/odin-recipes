Chashu Pork

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs of pork belly
  • 2 tbsp of neutral cooking oil
  • 1 cup of soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup of sugar
  • 1/2 cup of sake
  • 1/4 cup of honey
  • 3 cups of water
  • 2-3 scallions
  • 1 7-oz. piece of ginger
  • 2 cloves of garlic

Steps

  1. Leave the pork belly to go to room temperature (about 30 minutes)
  2. Salt and pepper the pork belly, then wrap in cling wrap and tenderize with a mallet
  3. Roll into a cylindrical shape
  4. Tie the pork belly tightly with cooking string so it holds its shape
  5. Heat the oil in a frying pan on medium-high heat and brown the pork belly until brown on all sides, take the pork off the heat and let rest
  6. Start a pot of water (just enough to cover the pork) and bring to simmer, add the pork belly, and cook on simmer for about an hour
  7. Drain the water when the hour is up
  8. Add the sugar, sake, soy sauce, water, scallions, garlic, and ginger to the pot and bring to boil then reduce to simmer for about an hour
  9. Skim the impurities about every 10-15 minutes
  10. At the end of the hour, turn the heat off and let the pot rest
  11. Once it is cool, wrap the pork belly in cling wrap and refrigerate overnight
  12. Take out the pork, remove the cooking string, and slice the pork about 1/4 inch thick
  13. Add to Ramen
